Transcritique - Bakunin and Marx

Let's transcritique Bakunin and Marx! As premise, the transcritique means philosophical approach produced by Kojin Karatani that method uses each to critique the other. Bakunin and Marx, they are often seen as opposing axis like a strife in the First International but I wanna integrate them because I think they have common points.

I try to critique Marx from perspective of Bakunin, Marx's strategy is authoritarian and centralized and he ignores people's spontaneity. However, I try to critique Bakunin from perspective of Marx, his theory is ethical but rough because it's not based on historical materialism and condition of the relations of production.

It does not mean that all Marxists is enemy of anarchist. Take for example, the Libertarian Marxism is libertarian(anti-authoritarian) interpret of Marxism. They importance role of individual free will in historical materialism and understand proletarian dictatorship as bottom up democratic power by the people, so not always conflict with anarchism.

What's next era of the MODERN?

The surmount of modern, that is difficult main theme of my and many orthodox socialists' thoughts. I can say modern is progressive and free era by comparison with medieval feudalism or ancient empire, but we shall not stop at modern. We have to defeat national-state, capitalism and patriarchy, these social structure that let people be alienation. Karl Marx images "Free association of producers" that will be alternative while critique of capitalism. I very agree that clear vision.

But I think political revolution and proletarian dictatorship is too authoritarian strategy. In fact, Soviet-Russia that's born by people's spontaneity but corrupt to state-capitalism with Bolsheviks. I'm oriented to economical revolution like Proudhon. I think the economical revolution needs decentralized technical methods like a P2P I'm working, to reform means of production and to establish a new society that's based on mutual aid by free will of each individuals.
